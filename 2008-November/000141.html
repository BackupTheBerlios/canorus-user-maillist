Interesting discussion. The call for an open source and mature "1.0" GUI 
score editor that can compete with the well known products from the 
commercial world (Finale, Sibelius) has been around in forums for a long 
time. At this moment canorus, nted, and mscore (Muse Score) are the most 
interesting projects. I have installed them all on my system and 
frequently download and compile the latest sources for betatesting-fun.

But when I have to work on a serious project I go back to my 
never-crashing texteditor and find myself typing the powerful and 
mysterious codes that are needed to produce my beautiful scores with 
abcm2ps, lilypond, and mup. The results are great. And once you get used 
to this way of working, it isn't that difficult really.

Every time I tried to prepare a serious score with nted, canorus, or 
mscore I bumped into a program crash, or a missing feature. My favorite 
"score editor" on my Linux box is therefore still: Vim :-)

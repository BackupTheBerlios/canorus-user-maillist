Thanks for telling me about MUP! I've been reading the documentation
and playing with the trial version, and I'm pretty impressed. As far
as text-based entry on mini-laptops goes, I think you're right...it is
ideal, if one can learn the syntax and everything. Also, I definitely
recommend the EEE! It's not a computing powerhouse, but it's a great
ultra-mobile device for basic computing!

Back to MUP...I think the included editor Mupmate is pretty
cool...it's nice to have an editor optimized for the syntax. My main
issues with using MUP full time for composing are with midi playback.
As a composer, it would be nice to be able to play back, say, just a
few measures in the middle of a piece I'm working on, instead of the
whole thing. I think that would get pretty annoying once I start
working on longer pieces! Are there any workarounds to this
limitation? An idea I had was making a "test" file just for measures
I'm working on, and then copying and pasting that into my "complete
work" file. How do you do it?

also, anybody willing to help get NoteEdit installed on my EEE? Not
sure if can somehow work around the kde-libs dependency.

Thanks!

> Personally I would not recommend Canorus. It's still too much in
> beta/alpha stage. Promising, but far from ready.
>
> The most reliable music scoring software for Linux are text based apps.
> Probably the best of them is Lilypond.
> A simpler alternative is abcm2ps. Smaller in size and requirements, easier
> syntax, and still with surprisingly good results.
> My personal favorite is Mup (http://www.arkkra.com). This can be placed
> somewhere between Lilypond and Abcm2ps. Easier to use than Lilypond, yet
> more powerful and flexible than abcm2ps. (Shareware)
>
> Text based Music score apps are IMO ideal for a mini laptop like the EEE
> PC. (I have been thinking of getting me one for that purpose. Can you
> recommend it ?). This kind of sofware does take some time to get used to
> though.
>
> With Canorus you can combine the ease of use of a graphical interface with
> the typesetting power of Lilypond (and maybe abcm2ps and mup in the future
> ?). I told you it is not ready yet, bu try it anyway. Post your
> bugreports, feature requests. The more active and critical canorus users,
> the faster canorus will evolve and improve.

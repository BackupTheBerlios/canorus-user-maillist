Hi David,

David Cuny pravi:
> >From the CLI, how do I access the pitch information of the notes? I can get do something like this:
>
>    -- get the first sheet
>    theSheet = document.sheetAt(0)
>
>    -- get the first staff in the sheet
>    theStaff = theSheet.staffAt(0)
>
>    -- get the first voice on the staff
>    theVoice = theStaff.voiceAt(0)
>
>    -- get the notes in the first voice
>    theNotes = theVoice.getNoteList()
>
> However, no MIDI pitch (or any sort of pitch) doesn't seem to be easily accessible from the objects getNoteList() returns.
>
>   
CANote has diatonicPitch() method which returns a pair noteName+accs.
You can also call getChord() on that note or globally to gather all
notes playing together at that time.
> Also, is there a way to insert chord information? For example, I'd like to write a script that automatically thickens a line to 4 part harmony based on the function of the note (based on its approach) and the current chord. I'd like to add this as an annotation to the staff, such as "CMaj7" or "Fm9". 
>   
> I could do this with CAText, but it's such a common thing in music, I'm surprised there isn't a dedicated class for this (and fingering diagrams). Am I looking in the wrong place?
>   
Chords and frets aren't implemented yet, sorry. It's on our todo list.
